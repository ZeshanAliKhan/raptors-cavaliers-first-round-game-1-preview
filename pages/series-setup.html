Series setup

How the series is actually set up before Game 1.

This is the 4-vs-5 matchup in the Eastern Conference first round. Cleveland opens at home as the No. 4 seed. Toronto arrives as the No. 5 seed after locking its place on the final day of the regular season.

The seed line

The seed line matters because it explains the floor order. Cleveland hosts Game 1 at Rocket Arena. Toronto has to prove immediately that the regular-season edge can survive a playoff opener on the road.

Why Game 1 matters more than the 3-0 record

The season sweep is the first thing people notice, but Game 1 is where the series gets rewritten. A strong Cleveland opener can turn the regular-season record into old information very quickly. A poised Toronto road performance can make the 3-0 edge feel predictive again.

The useful bracket read

Cleveland's side

Higher seed, home floor, first chance to reset the matchup.

Toronto's side

Lower seed, recent clinch momentum, and the cleaner head-to-head record.

Why it is interesting

The bracket gives Cleveland the better frame. The season series gives Toronto the better memory.
